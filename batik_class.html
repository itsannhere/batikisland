Batik is a traditional dyeing method that utilizes the wax-resist technique to color either the entire fabric or specific parts of it. The process involves applying wax to the fabric in the form of dots or lines using a tool called a 'Canting,' or using a copper stamp known as a 'Cap' to imprint wax onto the fabric. The areas without wax are dyed, and then the wax is partially removed using hot water, allowing for multiple rounds of dyeing. This intricate method requires precision and care, as the balance between applying wax and dye is essential to achieving the desired visual outcome.

Due to the detailed nature of the batik process, it is both time-consuming and demands a high level of focus to create the final artistic effect.

In the Batik Painting Healing Class, you'll be guided through the fundamental techniques of batik painting, including how to use the traditional "Canting" tool. The relaxed environment encourages small talk and creative work to help clear your mind from any cluttered thoughts.
