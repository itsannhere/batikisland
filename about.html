About Batik



Batik is a traditional Indonesian art form that involves dyeing fabric using a wax-resist technique. The process of creating batik designs is intricate and beautiful, with patterns ranging from simple geometric shapes to highly detailed depictions of nature, culture, and mythology.

In Indonesia, batik holds deep cultural significance and is often worn during important ceremonies, celebrations, and daily life. Each region in Indonesia has its own distinctive batik style, such as Batik Solo and Batik Yogyakarta, known for their rich browns and blacks, or Batik Pekalongan, which is more colorful and lively.
